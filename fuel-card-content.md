# Best Fuel Credit Cards in India: Which Card at Which Spend

## What you actually spend on fuel

| Vehicle | Typical monthly fuel bill |
|---------|--------------------------|
| Two-wheeler (daily 15-20 km commute) | ₹2,000-3,500 |
| Small car (hatchback, city commute ~30 km/day) | ₹4,000-6,000 |
| Sedan/SUV (city + weekend drives) | ₹6,000-10,000 |
| Long commute or highway regular | ₹10,000-15,000 |
| Multiple vehicles / fleet | ₹15,000+ |

If you're spending ₹3,000+ on fuel every month and swiping a general rewards card, you're leaving money on the table. A dedicated fuel card earns 3-5x more on every fill compared to a regular 1% cashback card.

## How we calculated

Formula: **Net annual savings = Annual fuel rewards + Milestone bonuses - Annual fee**

Joining fee is excluded since it's a one-time Year 1 cost. All fees shown include 18% GST. All numbers below are from the Great.Cards CardGenius engine.

## Fuel surcharge waiver: the extra saving most people miss

When you swipe a credit card at a petrol pump, the oil company charges a 1% surcharge on the transaction. Most fuel cards waive this. It's not a huge amount, but it adds up.

| Card | Surcharge Waiver | Transaction Range | Monthly Cap |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|------------|
| Indian Oil RBL XTRA | 1% | ₹500 - ₹4,000 | ₹200/mo |
| Kotak IndianOil | 1% | ₹100 - ₹5,000 | ₹100/mo |
| SBI BPCL | 1% | Up to ₹4,000 | ₹100/cycle |
| IDFC FIRST Power Plus | **None** | - | - |
| ICICI HPCL Coral | 1% | Varies | Varies |

At ₹5,000/month fuel (split across 2-3 fills), the surcharge waiver saves an additional ₹600-₹1,200/year depending on the card. This is on top of the reward points.

The IDFC Power Plus does not have a surcharge waiver. It compensates with a higher reward rate (5% on HPCL) but if you frequently fill small amounts at HPCL, factor in the missing waiver.

Your fuel card should match where you fill. A BPCL card at an IndianOil pump earns base rewards, not the accelerated rate.

## Three-card strategy for ₹15K+ spenders

If you're spending ₹15,000+ on fuel and hitting reward caps, consider splitting across cards:

| Card | Use for | Monthly fuel allocation | Annual savings |
|------|--------|----------------------|---------------|
| Indian Oil RBL XTRA | First ₹10K at IndianOil | ₹10,000 | ~₹7,230 |
| IDFC FIRST Power Plus | Next ₹3-4K at HPCL | ₹4,000 | ~₹1,811 |
| Kotak IndianOil Platinum | Overflow / any pump | Remainder | ₹960+ |

Total annual savings from fuel alone: ₹10,000+, versus ₹0 if you're swiping a basic rewards card.

This only makes sense if you have the discipline to route your fills. If you want one card and don't want to think about it, the RBL XTRA handles up to ₹12-13K/month before hitting caps.
